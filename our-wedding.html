Travel and Transportation

Getting from the ceremony to the reception:

Tom’s Town is about a half mile from the library: the library is at 11th and Main, and the distillery is at 17th and Main. It’s a walkable distance if you’re in reasonable shoes, and ubers and lyfts are also a good option. The most exciting transportation choice is the KC Streetcar, which runs up and down Main Street and is free! It is public transportation, so this option is slightly unreliable, but you bet Ellen and Dru are gonna try to hitch a ride.


Should I rent a car?

You don’t need to; there are plenty of ubers and lyfts in KC. But, renting cars can be pretty cheap in the midwest, so it might be a good option if you’re traveling with a group. Parking isn’t too difficult in KC.


Where do I fly into?

The Kansas City airport, MCI. It’s about 30 minutes outside of downtown; you’ll literally fly over cows when you land. Don’t let that scare you! Also, don’t let the airport itself scare you: it hasn’t been updated since the 70s. Southwest and Delta offer nonstops from New York to KC, and there are more options if you’re coming from LA. We usually fly Southwest.

Things to Do

Barbecue:

Joe’s of KC - Located in a gas station. Anthony Bourdain “discovered” this place, so lines can be long during peak hours.
Arthur Bryant's - Where the Presidents go when they visit KC Gates - A favorite local chain.
Q 39 - Maybe the best barbecue in the city. Make a reservation!

Local brews:

Tom’s Town - It was founded by Ellen’s uncle Steve, and the booze is tasty. Take a tour!
Boulevard Brewery - Get there early if you want to take a tour, or just hang in the tasting room. Dru’s fave is the Tank 7.
KC Bier Co.
Roasterie Coffee
Mildred’s Coffee

Jazz:

On Friday, we think that you should see Lonnie McFadden play at The Phoenix. He plays the trumpet, sings, and puts on a SHOW. Starts at 4:30, and getting there at 4 to get a seat is not a bad idea.
Green Lady Lounge has jazz every single night. It’s underground and very cool – and there’s no cover.
18th and Vine is the historic jazz district, and The Blue Room is a combo jazz museum and venue.

Cool Neighborhoods:

Crossroads - visit local galleries, boutiques, and coffee shops. Ellen loves Birdies!
Westside - artsy folk moved here when the Crossroads got too expensive.
Westpor - check out Broadway Cafe, a local staple that put the Starbucks on the corner out of business.
Country Club Plaza - the first ever planned shopping neighborhood in the country, it’s modeled after Sevilla. Pretty to walk around.
