Travel and Transportation

Getting from the ceremony to the reception:

Tom’s Town is about a half mile from the library: the library is at 11th and Main, and the distillery is at 17th and Main. It’s a walkable distance if you’re in reasonable shoes, and ubers and lyfts are also a good option. The most exciting transportation choice is the KC Streetcar, which runs up and down Main Street and is free! It is public transportation, so this option is slightly unreliable, but you bet Ellen and Dru are gonna try to hitch a ride.

Should I rent a car?

You don’t need to; there are plenty of ubers and lyfts in KC. But, renting cars can be pretty cheap in the midwest, so it might be a good option if you’re traveling with a group. Parking isn’t too difficult in KC.

Where do I fly into?

The Kansas City airport, MCI. It’s about 30 minutes outside of downtown; you’ll literally fly over cows when you land. Don’t let that scare you! Also, don’t let the airport itself scare you: it hasn’t been updated since the 70s. Southwest and Delta offer nonstops from New York to KC, and there are more options if you’re coming from LA. We usually fly Southwest.
